FAQ

Why did you make SkyAlt?
Few years back, I tried to find better way(than files&folders) how to organize my data. My requirements were simple:
- Cheap: I will use it for few decades
- Scale: We're talking terabytes here
- Open source: It must run, If company goes out of business
- Private: Only I can see my data
 I've made a list of ~20 products in the space. Most of them were SaaS or they were expensive. Out of luck, I've built one.

What are the use-cases?
SkyAlt is a database, which is easy to use like a spreadsheet. You can put anything into it. You can use it in your free time as well as at the office. Few examples: write notes, create a todo list, sort files, set reminders, store recipes, build CRM.

Do you privide hosting/cloud?
No. Right now, my focus is on building best tool for organizing data. I wanna have stable and useful tool, then I will keep adding services around it.

How much privacy do I have?
I started building SkyAlt for myself and I always want to use software which is open-source and unlimited. Software where sensitive data(financials, budgets, customer lists, etc.) are safe. No spyware. No backdoors. No Ads. No lock-in. You own & control your data. And that is SkyAlt. I don't have your data and can't access it. You can check the source-code.

Is it Open-source?
You can check 100% of code on GitHub. The code is released under Bussiness Source License, which gives you access to the source code and guarantees of converting to GPL after 5 years. I recognize that organizing your life's data will cost you a lot of time and energy. Thanks to source-available and self-hosting, you can be sure, you will never lose what you've built in SkyAlt.

How do you make money?
Right now, SkyAlt is free for everyone. In time I will activate Enterprise tier for commercial companies. Consumers will stay in the free tier. There will be only one, fully-featured and unlimited(#projects, #tables, #columns, #records, etc.) version for everyone.

Who are your competitors?
If you don't mind SaaS, you should try Airtable, Monday, Notion. Do you want on-premise? Try MS Access or FileMaker.

How can I help?
You can report a bug and request new features on GitHub or send me an e-mail.
